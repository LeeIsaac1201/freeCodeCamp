Stephen William Hawking

The man who unlocked the mysteries of the universe.

Timeline

  • 1942 - Born in Oxford, England.
  • 1962 - Graduates in physics from the University of Oxford and begins cosmology research at Cambridge.
  • 1963 - Diagnosed with ALS, a degenerative motor-neurone disease.
  • 1965 - Marries Jane Wilde.
  • 1966 - Completes his doctorate and receives a fellowship at Cambridge.
  • 1967 - First son, Robert, is born.
  • 1970 - Daughter Lucy is born. Develops theories on singularities in collaboration with Roger Penrose.
  • 1971 - Discovers the four laws of black hole mechanics with James Bardeen and Brandon Carter.
  • 1974 - Proposes the theory of Hawking radiation; elected as a Fellow of the Royal Society.
  • 1980 - Calculates quantum fluctuations during cosmic inflation, explaining galaxy distribution.
  • 1983 - Publishes the Hartle–Hawking state model, suggesting time did not exist before the Big Bang.
  • 1995 - Divorces Jane Wilde and marries Elaine Mason.
  • 2004 - Solves the Black Hole paradox.
  • 2007 - Divorces Elaine Mason.
  • 2009 - Receives the Presidential Medal of Freedom.
  • 2018 - Passes away at age 76.
